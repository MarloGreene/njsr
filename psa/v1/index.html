About PTSD

PTSD isn't weakness—it's your brain's alarm system stuck in the "on" position. The hypervigilance that kept you alive downrange? Now it fires at Walmart.

22% of combat veterans develop PTSD.

Complex PTSD

Multiple deployments can cause C-PTSD—regular PTSD plus difficulty controlling emotions and relationships. Your brain rewired to survive. That's not a character flaw.

Repeated trauma changes brain structure. The VA recognizes this.

Traumatic Brain Injury

Blast waves cause invisible damage. Symptoms overlap with PTSD: memory issues, irritability, headaches. Many vets have both—each is separately ratable.

TBI has its own VA rating, separate from PTSD.

Military Sexual Trauma

MST claims have special evidentiary standards—the VA knows reporting was often unsafe. Your word matters. Buddy statements and behavior changes count.

Talk to a VSO about MST-specific procedures.

Combat Loss & Survivor's Guilt

Losing a battle buddy isn't like other grief. The "why them not me" is a recognized trauma response that physically changes your brain.

Survivor's guilt is a documented PTSD symptom.
