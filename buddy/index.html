Before You Submit

  • Review the letter for accuracy - every statement should be truthful
  • Sign and date the letter
  • Include your contact information
  • Consider having it notarized for additional credibility
  • Keep a copy for your records

Writing Tips

Use "I observed" or "I witnessed" language rather than assuming you know what the veteran felt or thought.

What Makes a Strong Buddy Letter

  • Specificity: Concrete examples beat vague statements
  • First-hand: Only include what you personally witnessed
  • Timeline: Show when symptoms started and how they progressed
  • Observable: Focus on behaviors, not diagnoses
