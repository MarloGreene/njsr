Browser Fingerprinting Data

Privacy Warning: This data is visible to anyone viewing your screen. Do not share screenshots or display this page in public. It's for personal use only.

Browser fingerprinting collects unique characteristics of your browser and device to create a "fingerprint" that can identify you across websites without cookies. This includes hardware specs, software versions, and rendering behaviors. It's used for tracking, security, and analytics, but can raise privacy concerns.

Obfuscating Your Fingerprint

Browser fingerprints are hard to completely eliminate, but you can make yours less unique with these basic techniques:

  • Use privacy-focused browsers: Switch to Tor Browser, Brave, or Firefox with privacy extensions like uBlock Origin, HTTPS Everywhere, and NoScript.
  • Manage plugins and extensions: Disable unnecessary browser plugins or use a browser with fewer default plugins.
  • Adjust display settings: Change your screen resolution, zoom level, or browser window size to alter reported dimensions.
  • Modify fonts: Install or enable font blocking extensions to prevent font fingerprinting.
  • Use VPNs or proxies: Mask your IP address and location data, which can be part of fingerprinting.
  • Enable anti-fingerprinting features: Some browsers have built-in options to resist fingerprinting (e.g., Firefox's privacy.resistFingerprinting).
  • Clear cookies and data regularly: While not directly fingerprinting, it helps prevent tracking.

Note: These methods can impact usability and aren't foolproof. For strong privacy, consider using Tor or dedicated privacy tools.
