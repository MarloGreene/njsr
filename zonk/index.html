Steps to File Your VA Claim

1

Get Logged In

  • Go to va.gov and sign in (or create an account) using ID.me, DS Logon, or MyHealtheVet
  • ID.me typically has the fastest verification
  • You'll need: email, phone number, and your SSN

Pro Tip: Set up your account before you need it. The verification process can take 24-48 hours.

2

Grab Your DD-214

  • It's your golden ticket to prove your service
  • If you don't have it, request a copy at vetrecs.archives.gov
  • Keep multiple copies—digital and physical
  • Upload time: usually 7-10 days for online requests
3

Start Your VA Claim

  • On va.gov, click "File a Disability Claim"
  • Select the conditions you're claiming (e.g., PTSD, TBI, hearing loss, back pain)
  • Attach your DD-214 (that alone is enough to start!) and hit Submit
  • Don't wait—you can add more evidence later

Important: Your effective date (when payments start from) is the date you file, not when your claim is approved. File now, even if you don't have everything.

4

Gather Evidence (If You Have It)

Service Records:

  • Show deployments, combat, or other trauma events
  • DD-214, deployment orders, awards (CAB, CIB, Purple Heart)
  • Unit history, after-action reports if available

Buddy Letters:

  • Ask fellow veterans to describe what they saw (specifics help)
  • Include dates, locations, specific incidents
  • Doesn't need to be formal—authenticity matters more

Medical Records:

  • Include both military and civilian records
  • VA medical records automatically pulled by VA
  • Private doctor visits, therapy sessions, ER visits
  • Get a Nexus Letter from a doctor linking your condition to service
5

Attend Your C&P Exam

  • The VA will schedule a Compensation & Pension exam
  • Be honest—describe your worst days, not your best
  • Bring a list of symptoms and how they affect daily life
  • Don't downplay your symptoms (military stoicism works against you here)

Remember: This isn't a fitness test. The examiner needs to understand how your condition limits you.

6

Wait for Decision & Check Status

  • Check your claim status at va.gov
  • Average wait: 90-180 days (varies by region and complexity)
  • You'll receive a decision letter by mail
  • If approved, backpay goes to your original filing date
7

Appeal if Needed

  • If denied or rated lower than expected, you can appeal
  • File a Supplemental Claim (with new evidence) or Higher-Level Review
  • You have 1 year from decision date to appeal
  • Consider getting help from a VSO (Veterans Service Organization)—it's free

Common Service-Connected Conditions

Mental Health

  • PTSD
  • Depression
  • Anxiety
  • Adjustment Disorder

Brain & Nervous System

  • TBI (Traumatic Brain Injury)
  • Migraines
  • Tinnitus
  • Sleep Apnea

Musculoskeletal

  • Back pain
  • Knee injuries
  • Shoulder problems
  • Joint issues

Hearing & Vision

  • Hearing loss
  • Tinnitus (ringing)
  • Vision problems
  • Eye injuries

Note: Even if you never deployed, service-related injuries and conditions still qualify. That hypervigilance changed your brain—you started racking up brain injury hours the moment that drill sergeant got on your bus and started screaming at you to find those yellow footprints.

Pro Tips

✓ File Early: Your effective date is when you file, not when approved. File now, add evidence later.
✓ Be Specific: "I have back pain" is weak. "I wake up 3 times per night with lower back pain radiating to my right leg, limiting me to 30 minutes of standing" is strong.
✓ Document Everything: Keep copies of all submissions, letters, and correspondence.
✓ Describe Your Worst Days: Don't be stoic. The VA needs to understand how bad it really gets.
✓ Get Help: VSOs know the system. Use them—they're free and experienced.
✓ Don't Give Up: Many claims are initially denied or underrated. Appeal if needed.
